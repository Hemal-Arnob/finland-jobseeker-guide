Our website is designed to support all personnel arriving in Finland, as well as highly educated international professionals who are living in Finland. Here, you can find clear, practical, and up-to-date information to help you understand the Finnish job market and navigate your job search with confidence. Whether you are just arriving in Finland or already building your career here, this platform brings together the key resources you need in one place.

Job Search Tips:

  • Many Tech, Engineering, and IT jobs in Finland do not require Finnish, but Finnish language skills are always an advantage.
  • Learning basic Finnish can improve your job opportunities and help you at work and in daily life.
  • Apply through multiple websites to maximize your chances.
  • Keep your CV simple and clear, highlighting your key skills.
  • Ask employment services or integration programs for help when needed.

Finnish Language Learning

Explore a wide range of free, low-cost, and paid Finnish language courses across Finland, both in-person and online. Whether you are a beginner or want to improve your Finnish skills, these courses are structured to help you succeed.

Free or Low-Cost Courses

Startti Courses – Vantaa Adult Education Centre

Free Finnish Startti courses for immigrants and residents in Vantaa. Includes online options and Suomi Express courses for beginners and advanced learners.

Finnish Language Courses – International House Joensuu

Free courses for all levels in Joensuu. Registration via email. Beginners to advanced classes free of charge.

Open Finnish Lessons – Setlementti Puijola

Free Finnish classes and study groups; open to everyone, no registration needed (space permitting).

Keski-Pohjanmaan Kansanopisto Finnish Courses

Free beginner to intermediate courses in Central Ostrobothnia. Includes course materials, bus, and lunch for participants.

Live Lingua – Free Finnish Courses

Free online lessons with ebooks and audio resources for self-paced learning.

Learn Finnish Online – LearnFinnish.org

Interactive online lessons covering vocabulary, grammar, and culture. Free with optional paid materials.

Paid and Structured Courses

Finnish Courses Finder – Finnishcourses.fi

Course search portal listing Finnish classes across Finland. Filter by location, level, and price.

Finnish Online Courses – Gimara

Structured online classes (A2–B1) with assignments and regular schedule. Paid course.

Private and small-group lessons online with a professional teacher. Fee per package.

Lingvo Center Finland Language Courses

Group or online structured courses, paid. Includes online courses like “Suomi tutuksi”.

University Open Courses – University of Helsinki

Open university Finnish courses from basic to intermediate. Tuition waivers may apply for certain resident groups.

Job Qualification Cards & Certificates

In Finland, many jobs require official certificates or qualification cards showing you have completed training and passed a test for a specific skill or safety requirement. These are often mandatory in sectors like construction, logistics, transport, industry, hospitality, and technical services. Below you can find the most common certifications, with official links for training and registration.

Key Information about Job Certificates:

  • Also called Passports / Cards / Certificates, they officially prove competence in certain areas (safety, hygiene, equipment use).
  • Employers often require them before hiring, especially in hospitality, construction, logistics, and health-sensitive work.
  • Some certificates are valid permanently; others need renewal through refresher courses.
  • Training usually takes a few hours up to a day, followed by an official exam.

Common Job Qualification Cards in Finland

Hygiene Passport (Hygieniapassi)

Official certificate for handling perishable food in kitchens, cafés, restaurants, and food stores.

Alcohol Serving Passport (Anniskelupassi)

Mandatory for serving alcohol in licensed places. Learn laws and take a test.

Occupational Safety Card (Työturvallisuuskortti)

One-day course and exam. Valid for 5 years. Required for construction, industrial, and many workplaces.

First Aid Certificate (Ensiapukortti / EA-card)

Shows completion of basic first aid and CPR training, useful for workplaces including schools, offices, and construction.

Hot Work Card (Tulityökortti)

Required for jobs involving fire, sparks, heat, or welding. Covers safe hot work practices.

Forklift Safety Card (Trukkikortti)

Shows safe forklift operation. Needed in warehouses and logistics.

Water Work Card (Vesityökortti)

For plumbing and water maintenance jobs to prove safety and hygiene competence.

Road Safety Card (Tieturvakortti)

Shows competence in traffic safety at work sites, especially in road construction.

Security Guard Card (Järjestyksenvalvojakortti)

Required for security work. Covers law enforcement basics and safety.

Electric Work Safety Card (SFS 6002)

Official training in electrical safety for workplaces involving electrical work.

Work at Height / High Places Safety Card (Tieturva 1 & 2)

Covers safe practices for work at height and near traffic sites. Widely used in construction and roadwork.

Personnel Lift / Elevated Work Platform Card (Henkilönostinkortti)

Training for safe use of lifts and elevated platforms in construction, maintenance, and warehouses.

Fire Safety / Fire Extinguishing (Alkusammutus & AS1)

Short courses on basic fire safety and extinguisher use, often required by employers.

Heavy Vehicle / Driver Professional Qualification (ADR / Brake / Transport)

Certificates for professional driving and transport safety, including ADR, brake licence, and other vehicle handling qualifications.
